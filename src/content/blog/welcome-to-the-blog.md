Hello, and welcome. This is the inaugural post on a freshly-built personal blog that
runs entirely on the edge — every page is rendered ahead of time, pushed to Cloudflare's
global network, and served from whichever data center is closest to you.

## Why another blog?

Because writing in public is one of the highest-leverage things an engineer can do. It
forces clarity, archives your thinking, and — once in a while — helps someone halfway
across the world unblock themselves.

This blog is the workshop where I think out loud about:

- **Edge computing** — Workers, Durable Objects, D1, R2, and the rest of the Cloudflare stack
- **Web performance** — Core Web Vitals, INP, image optimisation, font loading
- **Developer experience** — type safety, tooling, build systems, and friction reduction
- **The craft of shipping** — the unglamorous middle 90% between "it works on my machine" and "it's in production"

## What you can expect

Posts will be infrequent but considered. No SEO-bait, no chatbot-generated filler, no
"10 reasons you should switch to X" — just things I've learned the hard way that might
save you a few hours.

Every post ships with:

- Full Open Graph + Twitter Card metadata, JSON-LD structured data
- A canonical URL, proper `lang` attribute, and sensible `robots` directives
- Reading time, tags, and a table of contents for longer reads
- An RSS feed at [`/rss.xml`](/rss.xml) — yes, RSS is still alive

If something here helps, I'd love to hear about it. The fastest way to reach me is via
email (see the [about page](/about)).

— ArtisanXL
