Everything starts with two words: *Hello, World!*

In 1972, Brian Kernighan picked this phrase while writing a tutorial. Billions of programmers since have typed the same two words while learning a new language or opening an empty file. PHP, Python, Rust, Go, whatever you open, the first example is always the same.

Not because it's simple. Something simpler could have worked: `print(1+1)` or `echo "test"`. But "Hello, World" is different. It's the machine saying hi to a human for the first time. When that sentence appears on the screen it says more than *"yes, this works."*

A connection gets made. Your code, the processor, your screen, they form a tiny loop for an instant. The first time you close that loop, something changes. I still remember writing `echo` into a black terminal in high school and watching the line come back at me. Sat with it for a while.

Same for this blog, honestly. There's a stack of stuff I've been meaning to dump into a file. Technical details are coming, Laravel, Cloudflare Workers, PHP performance, Astro... but these two words had to come first.

```php
<?php
echo "Hello, World!";
```

It works. Anyway, see you in the next one…
